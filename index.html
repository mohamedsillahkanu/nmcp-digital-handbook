Subnational Tailoring for Malaria Control

Overview

What is SNT?

Subnational Tailoring (SNT) is the process of adapting national malaria program strategies to reflect the unique epidemiological, operational, and socioeconomic context of each district. SNT enables targeted, evidence-based interventions that maximize impact with available resources.

Why SNT Matters

Districts vary widely in malaria burden, population density, health system capacity, and geography. One-size-fits-all approaches miss opportunities for efficiency. SNT uses local data to prioritize interventions and allocate resources where they will have the greatest effect.

SNT Cycle

Continuous process: Data Assembly → Analysis → Strategy → Implementation → Monitoring → Review. Each district completes this cycle quarterly, with annual comprehensive strategic reviews informing national-level policy.

Data Collection

Core Data Sources

Primary data: HMIS facility reports, DIS surveillance, case investigations. Secondary: population surveys, health facility assessments, mapping data. Tertiary: campaigns (ITN, IRS, SMC), treatment coverage.

Data Quality Assurance

Verify completeness, accuracy, and consistency. Use DQA tools to identify issues. Train health workers on proper documentation. Establish feedback loops for rapid correction. Review data at facility, district, and national levels.

Timeliness

Monthly HMIS reporting with district review. Quarterly SNT data assembly. Annual strategic reviews. Real-time alerts for outbreaks or supply gaps. Establish clear deadlines and accountability mechanisms.

ICF Collect Integration

Use mobile forms for field data collection. Real-time submission to DHIS2. Offline capability for areas with poor connectivity. Cascading dropdowns and validation rules reduce errors.

Analysis

Epidemiological Analysis 📊

Calculate district-level malaria burden: incidence, parasite positivity rate (TPR), age-stratified cases, seasonal patterns. Compare to national targets and neighboring districts. Identify geographic hotspots using facility-level mapping.

Dashboarding

Real-time HMIS Dashboards

Display monthly HMIS trends, facility-level performance, case mapping, and intervention coverage. Enable district staff to monitor progress toward targets in real time. Update automatically from DHIS2.

SNT District Profile Dashboard

Comprehensive district profile: epidemiology, interventions, capacity, and financial performance. Updated quarterly. Shared with national program, WHO, and partners. Includes trend analysis and comparison metrics.

Campaign-Specific Dashboards

Real-time tracking of ITN distribution, IRS campaigns, treatment campaigns. School-based distribution monitoring with QR codes. Staff ID card systems for accountability. Coverage mapping and progress toward targets.

Visualization Best Practices

Use choropleth maps for geographic patterns. Time series for trends. Bar charts for district comparisons. Keep visuals simple and actionable. Label clearly. Target audience: district health teams and national program.
